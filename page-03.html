Infinite Loops & Conditions

When loops never stop

If the condition in a while loop never becomes False, the loop runs forever. This is called an infinite loop. It's the most common beginner mistake with while loops — and it's always caused by the condition.

The rule of thumb

while loops require you to manage the variable yourself. Before you run any while loop, ask:

  1. Is the variable used in the condition being changed inside the loop?
  2. Is it changing in the right direction (towards False)?
  3. Will it actually reach the stopping value, or jump past it?

If all three answers are yes, your loop will terminate correctly.
