Repetition is the heart of programming

Programming is about automating repetition

At its core, programming means teaching a computer to do things automatically — especially things that need to happen many times. Imagine asking the computer to print "Hello" 1,000 times. You could type it 1,000 times, or you could write a loop.

Python gives you two main types of loops:

  • while — repeats while a condition is true. You decide what makes it stop.
  • for — steps through a sequence of values automatically. Python counts for you.

Both are powerful. Choosing which to use comes down to the problem at hand — and you'll learn exactly how over the next pages.
